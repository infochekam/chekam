# **1\.  Purpose and Scope**

## **1.1  Purpose**

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the end-to-end process that every Chekam Field Inspector must follow when conducting an on-ground property inspection on behalf of a diaspora client. It ensures that inspections are consistent, thorough, professionally documented, and delivered to the client in a timely manner.

## **1.2  Scope**

This SOP applies to all Chekam Field Inspectors and Coordinators involved in:

* Receiving and processing inspection assignments

* Scheduling and executing on-site property visits

* Capturing photo, video, and written evidence

* Completing inspection reports and quality checks

* Conducting title / dispute searches (where commissioned)

* Delivering results to the Virtual Inspector and ultimately to the client

## **1.3  Service Tiers Covered**

| Service | What It Includes | Trigger |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Virtual Inspection & Quality/Value Report | Video walkthrough \+ written condition & value report | Client books standard inspection |
| Root of Title Search | Legal verification of title documents & ownership chain | Client adds title search |
| Dispute Search | Legal check for active claims or encumbrances | Client adds dispute search |
| Comprehensive Virtual Inspection & Due Diligence | All of the above combined in one package | Client books full due-diligence package |

# **2\.  Roles and Responsibilities**

| Virtual Inspector (VI) | Client-facing agent who handles the pre-inspection call, gathers client requirements, liaises with the Field Inspector, and delivers the final report to the client via video call. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Field Inspector (FI)** | On-ground agent who physically visits the property, captures evidence (photos, video, measurements), fills all on-site checklists, and submits raw data to the VI within the agreed turnaround window. |
| **Inspection Coordinator (IC)** | Back-office role that receives client bookings, assigns FI to jobs, tracks progress, follows up on overdue submissions, and quality-checks all reports before client delivery. |
| **Legal Researcher (LR)** | Conducts title and dispute searches at land registries and relevant government offices. Operates independently but reports findings to the IC / VI. |
| **Quality Assurance (QA)** | Reviews completed reports against this SOP before client release. Signs off or returns for revision. |

# **4\.  Pre-Inspection Process**

This section covers all steps that must be completed BEFORE the Field Inspector sets foot on the property.

## **4.1  Receiving the Assignment**

1. Inspection Coordinator receives the client booking via the Chekam platform or designated channel.

2. IC logs the booking in the Assignment Register with the following details:

   * Client full name and contact

   * Property address and GPS coordinates

   * Service tier requested (Standard / Comprehensive)

   * Client-stated priorities from the Pre-Inspection Call (see Section 5\)

   * Agreed inspection date and delivery deadline

3. IC assigns the nearest available and qualified Field Inspector within 24 hours of booking confirmation.

4. FI receives the assignment notification and the client's Property Inspection Brief (PIB).

5. FI acknowledges receipt and confirms availability within 2 hours of notification.

# **5\.  Pre-Inspection Call — Virtual Inspector Script & Guidelines**

The Pre-Inspection Call is conducted by the Virtual Inspector (VI) before any physical inspection. Its goal is to build rapport with the client, gather precise requirements, and complete the Property Inspection Brief (PIB).

## **5.1  Call Structure (WHO – WHY – WHEN – WHAT – WHERE – HOW)**

| Step | Focus Area | Key Actions |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| WHO | Confirm client identity | Confirm full name, WhatsApp number, Chekam account reference |
| WHY | Understand motivation | Rental, purchase or investment? Urgency level? Living abroad — which country? |
| WHEN | Timeline & availability | Desired move-in or decision date? Available for live walkthrough? |
| WHAT | Property requirements | Type, size, floors, amenities, non-negotiables, budget |
| WHERE | Location preferences | Area in Lagos, proximity to work/school/hospital, transport links |
| HOW | Process & expectations | Explain Chekam's process, set delivery expectations, confirm service tier |

## **5.2  Sample Qualifying Questions by Criteria**

### **Location**

* What area in Lagos would you prefer? Are there areas you want to avoid?

* How important is proximity to your place of work, school, or other key locations?

* Do you have family or friends nearby who you would like to be close to?

### **Budget**

* Do you have a price range in mind for rent or purchase?

* Is the budget strict, or is there some flexibility?

* If a property is slightly above or below your budget but meets your other needs, would you consider it?

### **Property Type & Amenities**

* Are you looking for a flat, duplex, bungalow, or are you open to different types?

* How many bedrooms and bathrooms do you need?

* Are there any must-have features: constant water supply, parking space, security/gated estate, generator, swimming pool, smart home features, eco-friendly design?

### **Accessibility & Family Needs**

* Will elderly family members or people with mobility challenges be living in the property?

* Do you have young children? If so, is there a school or daycare you need to be near?

* Is access to a major road or public transport important?

### **Purpose**

* Is this property for your own use when you return, for a family member currently in Nigeria, or purely as an investment?

* If for investment, are you looking for rental yield, capital appreciation, or both?

## **5.3  Closing the Pre-Inspection Call**

10. Summarise the client's requirements back to them for confirmation.

11. Explain what happens next: 'Our field agent will visit the property on \[date\]. We will schedule a live walkthrough with you on \[date/time\] and deliver the full written report by \[deadline\].'

12. Confirm the client's preferred video platform for the live walkthrough (WhatsApp, Zoom, Google Meet).

13. Confirm payment status. Do not proceed to schedule inspection if payment is not confirmed.

14. Thank the client and send a WhatsApp message summary of agreed details within 30 minutes of the call.

## **6.10  Photography & Video Standards**

All media must meet the following minimum standards before leaving the site:

* Minimum 40 photographs for a standard inspection; minimum 60 for a comprehensive inspection.

* Every room must have at least 4 photos: wide-angle full room, flooring close-up, ceiling, and a feature detail (e.g. window, socket, damaged area).

* All photos must be taken in landscape orientation.

* Ensure photos are well-lit. Open all curtains and turn on lights before photographing.

* Label photos on your device immediately after capture: \[RoomName\]\_\[Feature\]\_\[Photo\#\] — e.g. Kitchen\_Sink\_01.

* Record one continuous 360° video of every room (minimum 30 seconds per room).

* Record an exterior walkthrough video (minimum 90 seconds covering all sides of the building).

* Record a neighbourhood overview video (minimum 60 seconds) showing road, surroundings, access points.

**⚠  Do NOT apply any Instagram or beauty filters to inspection photos. All images must be raw and accurate.**

**⚠  Do NOT delete any photos on site — include even unflattering images of defects. Selective photography is a ground for disciplinary action.**

## **6.11  Defects Documentation Protocol**

Any defect observed must be documented as follows:

21. Photograph the defect from at least 2 angles: context shot (shows location in room) and close-up.

22. Note the defect in the inspection form under the 'Defects Register' section: Location, Type, Severity (Minor / Moderate / Major), Estimated Cost to Fix (give range in Naira).

23. If a defect is structural (e.g. large cracks in load-bearing walls, severe water damage), flag it in the form as URGENT and notify the IC immediately by phone.

# **7\.  Live Virtual Walkthrough Call**

In some cases, the client may request a live video call during the inspection, or a follow-up live walkthrough using recorded media. Follow this protocol.

## **7.1  Preparing for the Live Call**

24. Confirm the call date, time, and platform with the client's Virtual Inspector at least 24 hours in advance.

25. Ensure your device is fully charged, internet connection is stable, and you are physically at or near the property.

26. Have the PIB open for reference during the call.

27. Test audio and video before the client joins. Use earphones where possible to reduce ambient noise.

## **7.2  During the Live Walkthrough**

28. The Virtual Inspector opens the call, introduces the Field Inspector to the client, and hands over to the FI.

29. FI greets the client professionally: 'Good morning/afternoon \[Client Name\], I am \[Name\] from Chekam. I am here at \[Address\] and will walk you through the property now.'

30. Begin outside — street view, entrance, compound — before entering the building.

31. Walk slowly and steadily. Keep the camera at eye level and avoid shaky movements.

32. Narrate as you walk: 'This is the living room. Flooring is ceramic tile in good condition. I will now show you the windows...'

33. Pause at features highlighted in the PIB as important to the client. Allow the client to ask questions.

34. Do NOT rush the walkthrough. A typical live walkthrough takes 30–60 minutes.

35. If the client asks a question you cannot answer (e.g. legal question, price negotiation), respond: 'That is a great question. I will note it and our team will provide a full answer in the written report.'

36. End the walkthrough with a summary: 'That concludes the walkthrough. Our full written report will be ready by \[deadline\].'

# **8\.  Inspection Report — Writing and Submission**

## **8.1  Report Turnaround Times**

| Service Tier | Report Deadline (from on-site visit) |
| :---- | :---- |
| Standard Virtual Inspection & Quality/Value Report | Within 24 hours |
| Root of Title Search | Within 48–72 hours (depends on registry) |
| Dispute Search | Within 48–72 hours |
| Comprehensive Virtual Inspection & Due Diligence | Within 72 hours (all components combined) |

## **8.2  Required Sections in the Inspection Report**

Every Chekam Inspection Report must contain the following sections in order:

37. **Cover Page:** Property address, client name, inspection date, Field Inspector name, service tier, Chekam logo.

38. **Executive Summary:** 3–5 bullet points summarising the most important findings — suitable for a client who only has 60 seconds.

39. **Property Overview:** Type, floor, age estimate, ownership structure (self-contained/compound), estate or standalone.

40. **Location Assessment:** Neighbourhood quality, road access, flood risk, proximity to key amenities (market, school, hospital, main road).

41. **Exterior Assessment:** Findings from Section 6.2 above, with photos embedded.

42. **Interior Assessment (Room by Room):** Findings for each room as per Sections 6.4–6.8, with photos embedded.

43. **Utilities & Infrastructure Assessment:** Findings from Sections 6.8–6.9, with photos.

44. **Defects Register:** Table listing all defects with severity, location, photo reference, and estimated repair cost in Naira.

45. **Value-for-Money Assessment:** FI/VI's professional opinion on whether the asking price is fair, above, or below market rate for the area. Include 2–3 comparable properties as reference where possible.

46. **Recommendation:** Clear statement: RECOMMENDED / RECOMMENDED WITH CONDITIONS / NOT RECOMMENDED. If conditional, list conditions clearly.

47. **Photo Gallery:** All labelled photos in grid layout (4 per row minimum).

## **8.3  Writing Standards**

* Use clear, plain English. Write as if explaining to a client who has never visited Lagos.

* Be objective and factual. Avoid language like 'nice' or 'looks good' — instead write 'flooring is in good condition with no visible cracks or chips.'

* All measurements must be stated in metres (e.g. '3.5m × 4m').

* All costs must be stated in Naira (₦) with an approximate USD equivalent in brackets.

* Every claim about a defect must be supported by at least one embedded photo.

* Spell-check the entire report before submission.

**⚠  Do NOT include the client's budget or personal details in the body of the report. This is a confidential document.**

## **9.2  Root of Title Search — Steps**

55. Legal Researcher receives the title documents from the IC.

56. LR visits the Lagos State Land Registry (or relevant state registry) to confirm:

    * The C of O or R of O is genuine and not forged.

    * The property has not been revoked by government.

    * The current named owner matches the seller.

    * There are no outstanding encumbrances (mortgages, liens).

57. LR obtains an official search report or stamped confirmation from the registry where possible.

58. LR documents all findings in the Title Search Report template.

59. LR submits the completed Title Search Report to IC within the agreed deadline.

*ℹ  Registry visits can be delayed due to queue or administrative issues. Always allow for 48–72 hours. If delays are expected beyond 72 hours, notify IC immediately so the client can be updated.*

## **9.3  Dispute Search — Steps**

60. LR searches the relevant courts (High Court, Magistrate Court) for any filed cases relating to the property address or the current owner's name.

61. LR checks with the Surveyor General's office for any acquisition or compulsory purchase notices affecting the land.

62. LR makes enquiries with the local community or compound head (Baale) where the property is a community or family land.

63. LR confirms whether any government road expansion or infrastructure development plans affect the property (check LASG or Federal Ministry of Works notices).

64. All findings are documented in the Dispute Search Report template with supporting evidence (court file references, official letters).
